Featured 01 · Fintech · Econometrics · Python
Cryptocurrency Pair Trading Strategy
Cointegration, ARDL & Causality Analysis — 10 Cryptos, 5 Pairs
  • Analyzed relationships between major cryptocurrency pairs to evaluate pair trading opportunities in volatile crypto markets.
  • Studied whether selected crypto pairs maintain long-term equilibrium relationships or short-term predictive interactions.
  • Focused on pairs such as BTC–ETH, XRP–XLM, DOT–LINK, BCH–LTC, and ADA–AVAX.
  • Goal was to identify data-driven trading opportunities using statistical and econometric models.
  • Collected historical cryptocurrency price data for selected pairs.
  • Conducted stationarity tests to check time-series stability.
  • Applied Engle-Granger Cointegration Test to examine long-term relationships.
  • Used Granger Causality Test to detect short-term predictive influence between cryptocurrencies.
  • Built OLS regression and ARDL models to analyze price dynamics and strength of relationships.
  • No strong long-term cointegration relationships were observed among the analyzed pairs.
  • Several pairs showed significant short-term predictive relationships.
  • XRP movements influence XLM, indicating strong short-term market linkage.
  • LINK showed predictive influence over DOT in certain periods.
  • Regression results revealed strong same-day correlations, suggesting potential short-term trading signals.
  • Long-term mean-reversion pair trading strategies may not be reliable for these cryptocurrencies.
  • However, short-term statistical trading strategies can be effective due to strong market volatility.
  • Data-driven analysis helps uncover predictive relationships and trading signals in crypto markets.
  • Highlights the importance of advanced econometric models for financial market analysis.

Featured 02 · Policy Analytics · GovTech · Python
Aadhaar Ecosystem Health
Assessing Operational Health of India's Aadhaar — IHS Framework
  • Conducted a data-driven evaluation of the Aadhaar service ecosystem across Indian states using government datasets.
  • Analyzed operational trends in enrolments, demographic updates, and biometric updates to identify system efficiency and service gaps.
  • Developed a composite Identity Health Score (IHS) to measure the operational health and maturity of Aadhaar services at the state level.
  • Aims to support policy decisions, operational planning, and improved citizen service delivery in India's digital identity infrastructure.
  • IHS combines multiple operational indicators using a weighted scoring model (0–100 scale).
  • Maintenance Ratio (40%) — measures sustainability of identity updates vs enrolments.
  • Service Entropy (40%) — evaluates balance across service types (enrolment, demographic, biometric).
  • Efficiency / Pressure Intensity (20%) — measures operational workload and citizen effort.
  • States classified into 4 tiers: Operational Leaders (IHS >61) / Steady Maintainers (51–61) / Emerging Systems (41–51) / Critical Intervention (≤41).
  • Aadhaar operations are shifting from new enrolments to lifecycle maintenance, indicating system maturity.
  • Compliance ratio strongly impacts IHS performance — higher compliance achieves better operational efficiency.
  • Pressure intensity and workload imbalance reduce service efficiency, especially in Tier 3 and Tier 4 states.
  • Tier 2 and Tier 3 states show higher citizen service time, indicating process delays and operational bottlenecks.
  • Aadhaar activity peaks between September and December, requiring better resource planning during high-demand periods.
  • Service balance, compliance, and workload management are key drivers of Aadhaar ecosystem performance.
  • The IHS framework provides a practical diagnostic tool for monitoring and improving digital identity operations across states.
  • Tier-based insights enable targeted policy interventions such as infrastructure support for weaker states.
  • Highlights how data analytics can enhance governance, operational efficiency, and citizen service delivery in large-scale digital systems.

Featured 04 · Aviation · Machine Learning · Python
Flight Delay Prediction & Optimization
Comprehensive Analysis of U.S. Aviation System Delays (2003–2016)
  • Conducted a comprehensive analysis of U.S. aviation flight delay data (2003–2016) to identify patterns, causes, and operational challenges affecting airline performance.
  • Analyzed delay drivers such as carrier issues, late aircraft, weather, and national aviation system disruptions.
  • Built a machine learning model to predict total monthly delay minutes and identify the most influential factors.
  • The goal was to provide data-driven insights to improve airline scheduling, airport efficiency, and delay management strategies.
  • Developed a Random Forest Regression model to predict total delay minutes.
  • Model used features such as total flights, previous delay ratios, month (seasonality), airport information, and carrier activity.
  • Achieved R² score of 0.80, explaining about 80% of the variation in flight delays.
  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 31,556 minutes, indicating strong predictive performance across all records.
  • Feature importance analysis revealed the most critical predictors affecting delay patterns.
  • Flight volume (70.6%) is the strongest predictor of total delays, indicating congestion when airport capacity is exceeded.
  • Previous month delay ratio (16.9%) shows a "carry-over effect" where earlier disruptions increase future delays.
  • Seasonality (5.2%) plays a role, with delays increasing during summer travel peaks and extreme weather periods.
  • Major hub airports ATL, ORD, and DFW contribute significantly to total delays due to heavy traffic volumes.
  • Delay distribution is highly skewed — a few extreme delay events account for a large share of total delay minutes.
  • Flight delays are primarily driven by system saturation and operational fatigue in the aviation network.
  • Machine learning insights highlight the importance of capacity management and proactive scheduling.
  • Strategies such as dynamic flight caps, buffer scheduling, and risk-based planning can reduce large-scale disruptions.
  • The study demonstrates how data analytics and predictive modeling can improve operational decision-making in aviation systems.

Comparative Evaluation of 7 Renewable Energy Types · Kaggle Dataset
  • Conducted a big data analytics study to evaluate the efficiency of multiple renewable energy technologies including solar, wind, hydro, biomass, geothermal, tidal, and wave energy.
  • Used a Kaggle renewable energy dataset containing project-level data such as installed capacity, energy production, storage efficiency, investment, and environmental impact.
  • Built an automated analytical workflow in KNIME to compute performance metrics, compare technologies, and identify efficiency patterns.
  • Objective was to support data-driven investment, operational optimization, and renewable energy policy planning.
  • Excel Reader Node — imported renewable energy dataset into KNIME for processing.
  • Rule Engine Node — converted coded values into readable renewable energy categories.
  • Math Formula Nodes — calculated key metrics: Net Energy, Efficiency Ratio, Performance Ratio, and Efficiency Score.
  • GroupBy Node — aggregated performance metrics by renewable energy type for comparison.
  • Normalizer Node — scaled variables to ensure fair comparison across metrics with different ranges.
  • K-Means Clustering Node — grouped renewable technologies based on efficiency and capacity patterns.
  • Bar Chart Nodes — visualized KPI comparisons across all 7 energy types.
  • Excel Writer Node — exported clustering results for further analysis and visualization.
  • Biomass and hydro energy showed the highest net energy output and strong operational efficiency.
  • Solar energy achieved the highest performance ratio, indicating superior energy conversion efficiency.
  • Wind and geothermal demonstrated moderate performance with opportunities for efficiency improvements.
  • Tidal and wave energy remain emerging technologies with limited deployment but strong future potential.
  • Cluster analysis: Hydro as high-capacity mature technology; Biomass, Tidal, and Wind as moderately developed; Solar, Geothermal, and Wave as emerging with lower installed capacity.
  • Net Energy (MWh) — actual usable energy produced after internal energy consumption.
  • Performance Ratio (%) — efficiency of converting available resources into electrical energy.
  • Efficiency Ratio (%) — operational efficiency of converting input energy to output energy.
  • Efficiency Score — composite metric combining storage capacity, storage efficiency, and production performance.

Project 07 · Healthcare · Clinical Statistics · Python
Clinical Statistical Analysis — Aloe Juice Study
Aloe Juice Treatment on Oral Condition in Cancer Patients
  • Conducted a clinical statistical analysis to evaluate the effect of Aloe Juice treatment on oral health in cancer patients undergoing therapy.
  • Dataset included 23 patients monitored over a 6-week period with repeated measurements of oral condition.
  • Patients were divided into two groups: Aloe Juice treatment and placebo to compare treatment effectiveness.
  • Aimed to determine whether Aloe Juice significantly improves oral conditions compared to placebo using statistical modeling and hypothesis testing.
  • Descriptive Statistics — summarized patient characteristics such as age, weight, cancer stage, and oral condition scores.
  • Independent Samples T-Test — compared oral condition scores between the Aloe Juice and placebo groups at Week 6.
  • Mixed-Effects Model (GLM) — analyzed repeated measurements to evaluate the effects of time, treatment, and their interaction.
  • Normality Testing (Kolmogorov-Smirnov) — checked data distribution to determine appropriate statistical tests.
  • Nonparametric Tests (Wilcoxon & Friedman) — evaluated improvement in oral condition across multiple time points.
  • Data Visualization — used graphs to analyze trends in oral condition scores over time.
  • Oral condition scores improved significantly over the six-week study period for patients overall.
  • No statistically significant difference was found between the Aloe Juice and placebo groups.
  • The mixed-effects model confirmed a strong time effect, indicating improvement due to recovery over time rather than treatment.
  • Nonparametric tests further confirmed significant improvement across weeks, supporting the time-based trend.
  • Graphical analysis showed similar improvement patterns for both groups, with overlapping confidence intervals.
  • Cancer patients showed significant improvement in oral condition over time, regardless of treatment type.
  • Aloe Juice treatment did not demonstrate a statistically significant advantage over placebo in improving oral health.
  • The findings highlight the importance of controlled clinical studies and statistical validation when evaluating complementary treatments.
  • Future studies with larger samples and longer observation periods may provide more definitive conclusions about Aloe Juice efficacy.
